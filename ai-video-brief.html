How I Cut Client Revisions by 70% Using a Structured AI Video Brief

If you edit video for clients, you already know the pain: a vague brief means five revision rounds, confused feedback, and scope creep that eats your margin.

I used to think the problem was my editing speed. It wasn't. The problem was the brief.

The Real Cost of a Bad Brief

Every ambiguous request costs you hours. "Make it pop" is not feedback. "The hook feels slow" is closer, but still incomplete. What actually works is a structured pre-production document that forces the client to decide on messaging, pacing, and deliverables before you ever open your timeline.

What Changed

I switched to a three-part system:

  • Client brief — one page that locks in goals, audience, and tone before filming starts.
  • Visual storyboard — thumbnail sketch + shot list so the client can see the narrative before edits begin.
  • Revision tracker — a shared doc with version numbers, timestamped feedback, and approval gates.

Revisions dropped from five rounds to two. Average project time fell by about forty percent.

AI video creators, short-form editors, freelance videographers, and anyone selling creative work by the project or retainer.
